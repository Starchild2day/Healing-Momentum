The supplement suggestions in this app are based on published nutritional and sports medicine research. However, every person is different. Drug Interactions

Some supplements (including fish oil, turmeric, and magnesium) may interact with prescription medications, blood thinners, or other supplements. If you are taking any prescription medication, consult your doctor before adding supplements to your routine.

Exercise Safety

All exercises should be performed within a pain-free range of motion. If any exercise causes sharp pain, numbness, tingling, or increased swelling, stop immediately and consult your healthcare provider. Do not use this app as a replacement for your prescribed physical therapy program.

About This Protocol

Research sources & important information

How These Protocols Were Developed

The exercise protocols, supplement recommendations, and nutrition guidance in this app are based on published research in physical therapy, sports medicine, orthopedic rehabilitation, and nutritional science.

Key sources include peer-reviewed studies from journals including the Journal of Bone and Joint Surgery, the American Journal of Sports Medicine, Arthritis & Rheumatology, and the Journal of Nutritional Biochemistry. Supplement recommendations are informed by clinical trials and systematic reviews available through the National Library of Medicine (PubMed).

Content was developed and organized with AI research assistance (Claude by Anthropic). This app is not affiliated with or endorsed by any medical institution or university.

Notable Research Referenced

Glucosamine + Chondroitin for knee cartilage: GAIT Trial (NIH-funded, New England Journal of Medicine, 2006) and multiple follow-up studies.

Curcumin vs. ibuprofen for knee pain: Randomized controlled trial, Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine, 2014.

Magnesium and sleep quality: Multiple studies including Abbasi et al., Journal of Research in Medical Sciences, 2012.

Fish oil and joint inflammation: Systematic reviews in Rheumatology (Oxford) and the British Journal of Nutrition.

Vitamin D3 and musculoskeletal health: Holick MF, New England Journal of Medicine, 2007; and multiple subsequent rehabilitation studies.

Collagen peptides and connective tissue repair: Shaw et al., American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 2017.

Important Disclaimer

This app is a wellness support tool, not a medical device or clinical treatment. Always consult your physician, physical therapist, or licensed healthcare provider before starting any new exercise or supplement program. Supplement suggestions may not be appropriate for everyone. Individual results will vary.

Healing Momentum and its creators assume no liability for any health outcomes resulting from use of this app. Use at your own risk.

Questions or Concerns

If you experience increased pain, new symptoms, or have questions about your specific situation, please consult your healthcare provider before continuing.
